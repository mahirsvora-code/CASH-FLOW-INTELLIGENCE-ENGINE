# Cash Flow Model

## Monthly formula
Closing Cash = Opening Cash + Inflows - Outflows

## Inflows
Inflows should include:
- Sales receipts
- Loan drawdowns
- Booking advances
- Any other project receipts

## Outflows
Outflows should include:
- Land payments
- Construction payments
- Contractor bills
- Material purchases
- Interest payments
- Approval and compliance payments

## Runway logic
- If closing cash stays above zero, the project is solvent for that month
- The first month with negative closing cash becomes the critical warning month
- The depth of negative cash equals the funding gap

## Scenario logic
If delay increases by N months:
- shift outflows forward where applicable
- delay sales receipts where applicable
- increase interest and overhead where applicable
- apply escalation to construction costs if needed

## Recommendation logic
If the shortfall is caused by timing, recommend:
- delay a phase
- shift payment milestones
- increase collections earlier
- reduce short-term spend
- reschedule procurement

If the shortfall is caused by weak sales, recommend:
- slow launch pace
- revise pricing
- adjust unit mix messaging

If the shortfall is caused by funding mismatch, recommend:
- renegotiate drawdown timing
- increase milestone funding
- reduce debt pressure in the early phase
